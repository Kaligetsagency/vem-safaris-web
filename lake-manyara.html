Lake Manyara National Park

The Pink Lake & Tree-Climbing Lions

Overview

Tucked at the base of the dramatic Great Rift Valley escarpment, Lake Manyara National Park is a scenic gem. Ernest Hemingway once described it as "the loveliest I had seen in Africa." The park is defined by a massive, shallow alkaline lake that expands and shrinks with the seasons, creating a unique habitat that supports a dense and diverse wildlife population in a relatively small area.

The Wildlife Experience

As you enter the park, you pass through a lush, groundwater forest where massive troops of baboons hang out by the roadside. Deeper in, the forest opens up to grassy floodplains where buffalo, wildebeest, and zebra graze. But the park is most famous for its predators—specifically, the legendary tree-climbing lions who spend their afternoons draped over the branches of mahogany and acacia trees to escape the heat and the insects.

Highlights & Activities

  • ✓ Tree-Climbing Lions: A rare behavior seen almost nowhere else in the world, making for an unforgettable photographic opportunity.
  • ✓ Flamingos & Birdlife: Depending on the water levels, the lake edge can be painted bright pink by thousands of flamingos. The park boasts over 400 species of birds.
  • ✓ The Hippo Pool: Watch massive pods of hippopotamuses wallowing in the water, grunting and socializing as waterbirds hitch rides on their backs.

Best Time to Visit

July to October: Best for large mammal viewing, including the lions and elephants.
November to June: The wet season is the absolute best time for bird watching, waterfalls, and seeing the massive flamingo flocks.
