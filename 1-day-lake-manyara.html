1-Day Lake Manyara Tour

The Rift Valley Jewel & Tree-Climbing Lions

Trip Overview

Nestled at the base of the dramatic Great Rift Valley escarpment, Lake Manyara National Park is a scenic gem. Ernest Hemingway once called it "the loveliest I had seen in Africa." This day trip offers an incredible diversity of habitats, from lush groundwater forests teeming with monkeys to the alkaline lake shores painted pink by thousands of flamingos.

Morning: Arusha to the Groundwater Forest

Your guide will pick you up from your Arusha hotel at 8:00 AM. After a scenic 2-hour drive through the Maasai Steppe and the colorful town of Mto wa Mbu, we enter the park gates. The adventure begins immediately in the dense groundwater forest, where you will encounter some of the largest troops of baboons in Africa, alongside blue monkeys and bushbucks.

Mid-Day: Lake Shores & Hot Springs Picnic

As the forest opens up into grassy floodplains, we approach the massive alkaline lake. Depending on the water levels, you can see huge flocks of flamingos, pelicans, and storks. We will enjoy a boxed picnic lunch at a designated site near the Maji Moto (hot springs), offering panoramic views of the lake and the rift valley wall.

Late Afternoon: The Search for Tree-Climbing Lions

In the afternoon, we focus our game drive on searching the acacia and mahogany trees. Lake Manyara is world-famous for its unique population of tree-climbing lions, who lounge on the branches to escape the heat and insects. Keep an eye out for giraffes, elephants, and hippos wallowing in the mud before we slowly make our way out of the park and return to Arusha by 6:00 PM.
