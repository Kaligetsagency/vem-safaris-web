Tarangire National Park

The Home of the Giants

Overview

Often referred to as the "Elephant Capital of the World," Tarangire National Park is a striking landscape defined by its rolling hills and the magnificent, ancient Baobab trees that dot the horizon. The park is named after the Tarangire River, which flows through its center and serves as the primary water source for wildlife during the dry season.

The Wildlife Experience

During the dry season, the park experiences a mini-migration of its own. Thousands of animals, including zebra, wildebeest, and cape buffalo, flock to the Tarangire River. However, the true stars of the show are the elephants. It is common to see herds of up to 300 elephants scratching the dry river bed for underground streams or stripping the bark off the baobab trees.

Highlights & Activities

  • ✓ Massive Elephant Herds: Enjoy incredibly close encounters with these intelligent, gentle giants as they roam freely.
  • ✓ The Ancient Baobabs: Also known as the "Tree of Life," these massive trees create a dramatic, prehistoric backdrop perfect for photography.
  • ✓ Birdwatching Paradise: With over 500 species of birds recorded in the park, including the beautiful Lilac-breasted Roller and flocks of colorful lovebirds, it is a birder's dream.

Best Time to Visit

June to October: The dry season is absolutely the best time to visit Tarangire. Because water is scarce everywhere else, massive numbers of animals migrate into the park and congregate around the river, making game viewing spectacular and easy.
