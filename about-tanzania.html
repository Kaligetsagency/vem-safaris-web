Discover Tanzania

The Heart of African Safari

Welcome to Tanzania, a land of staggering natural beauty, profound cultural heritage, and the most spectacular wildlife viewing on the planet. Whether you are scaling the heights of Mount Kilimanjaro or relaxing on the spice island of Zanzibar, Tanzania promises an unforgettable adventure.

Geography & Landscapes

Located in East Africa, Tanzania is bordered by the Indian Ocean to the east and the massive Great Lakes (Victoria, Tanganyika, and Malawi) to the north, west, and south. It is home to Africa's highest point (Mount Kilimanjaro) and its lowest point (the floor of Lake Tanganyika).

The northern safari circuit—where VEM Safaris operates—features the iconic endless plains of the Serengeti, the volcanic caldera of the Ngorongoro Crater, and the baobab-studded landscapes of Tarangire.

People & Culture

Tanzania is one of the most culturally diverse and peaceful countries in Africa, boasting over 120 distinct ethnic groups. Despite this immense diversity, the country is united by a strong sense of national identity and a shared language.

  • Language: Swahili (Kiswahili) and English are the official languages. English is widely spoken in tourist areas, but learning a few Swahili words like Jambo (Hello) and Asante (Thank you) will earn you massive smiles!
  • The Maasai: Perhaps the most famous of Tanzania's tribes, the semi-nomadic Maasai are known for their striking red shukas (robes), incredible jumping dances, and their deep connection to their cattle and the land.
  • Safety: Tanzanians are renowned for their warm hospitality and friendliness. It is considered one of the safest countries in Africa for travelers.

Climate & Weather

Tanzania sits just south of the equator, meaning it enjoys a tropical climate with relatively consistent temperatures year-round. Instead of four seasons, Tanzania has wet and dry seasons:

  • The Dry Season (June to October): The most popular time to visit. The weather is cooler, skies are clear, and wildlife is easy to spot as animals gather around scarce water sources.
  • The Short Rains (November & December): Brief, unpredictable showers that usually happen in the late afternoon. Excellent for bird watching and avoiding heavy crowds.
  • The Long Rains (March to May): Heavy afternoon downpours turn the landscape lush and green. This is considered the low season, but offers stunning photography opportunities and lower rates.

Practical Information

  • Currency: The Tanzanian Shilling (TZS). However, US Dollars are widely accepted in the tourism industry. Note: US bills must be printed after 2013, or they may be rejected.
  • Visas: Most nationalities require a visa. We highly recommend applying online for an e-Visa before you travel.
  • Time Zone: East Africa Time (EAT), which is GMT+3.
