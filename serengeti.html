Serengeti National Park

The Land of Endless Plains

Overview

The Serengeti is Tanzania’s oldest and most popular national park, and a world heritage site. Renowned for its wealth of leopards and lions, and famous for the Great Migration, it is the ultimate safari destination. The name "Serengeti" comes from the Maasai word Siringet, meaning "the place where the land runs on forever."

The Great Migration

Over two million wildebeest, zebras, and gazelles move through the Serengeti and Masai Mara ecosystems in search of green pasture. Witnessing the herds cross the crocodile-infested Grumeti or Mara rivers is one of the most thrilling wildlife spectacles on Earth. Our guides track the herds year-round to put you in the heart of the action.

Highlights & Activities

  • ✓ The Big Cats: The Serengeti is the best place in East Africa to see lions, cheetahs, and elusive leopards in their natural habitat.
  • ✓ Hot Air Balloon Safaris: Float silently above the plains at dawn, watching the wildlife below, followed by a champagne breakfast in the bush.
  • ✓ Year-Round Game Viewing: Even when the migration has moved, the resident wildlife (elephants, giraffes, hippos) ensures spectacular viewing at any time of year.

Best Time to Visit

June to October: The best time for general wildlife viewing and catching the dramatic river crossings in the north.
January to February: Calving season in the southern Ndutu region—excellent for seeing baby animals and intense predator action.
