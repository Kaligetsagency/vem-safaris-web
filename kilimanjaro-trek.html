7-Day Kilimanjaro Trek

Trip Overview: The Machame Route

Challenge yourself on the roof of Africa. The Machame Route, known as the "Whiskey Route," is considered the most scenic path up Mount Kilimanjaro. It offers stunning views and a longer climb, which allows for excellent altitude acclimatization and a much higher success rate for reaching Uhuru Peak.

Day 1: Machame Gate to Machame Camp

We depart Arusha and drive to the Machame Gate. After registration, we begin our trek through the dense, lush rainforest of Kilimanjaro's lower slopes to our first camp.

Day 2: Machame Camp to Shira Camp

We leave the rainforest behind and enter the moorland zone. The trail steepens as we cross a valley and climb a rocky ridge to the Shira Plateau, offering views of the Western Breach.

Day 3: Shira Camp to Lava Tower to Barranco Camp

A crucial day for acclimatization. We trek up to the Lava Tower (4,600m) for lunch, then descend into the beautiful Barranco Valley. "Walk high, sleep low" is the rule today.

Day 4: Barranco Camp to Karanga Camp

We start the day by tackling the famous Barranco Wall—a fun, non-technical scramble. We then cross a series of hills and valleys before resting at Karanga Camp.

Day 5: Karanga Camp to Barafu Camp

A shorter trek today takes us to Barafu Camp, our base camp for the summit. We rest, prepare our gear, and go to sleep very early in preparation for the midnight climb.

Day 6: Summit Day! (Barafu to Uhuru Peak to Mweka Camp)

We wake up at midnight and trek through the freezing darkness up steep scree. We reach Stella Point at dawn, then push on to Uhuru Peak (5,895m). After celebrating, we descend all the way down to Mweka Camp.

Day 7: Mweka Camp to Mweka Gate to Arusha

A gentle trek down through the rainforest to the park gate to receive your summit certificates. Our vehicles will be waiting to drive you back to Arusha for a well-deserved hot shower and celebration.
