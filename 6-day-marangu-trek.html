6-Day Marangu Route

The Classic "Coca-Cola" Hut Route

Trip Overview

The Marangu Route is the oldest and most established path to the summit of Mount Kilimanjaro. It is affectionately known as the "Coca-Cola" route because it is the only route that offers dormitory-style hut accommodations, meaning you don't have to sleep in a tent! Approaching from the southeast, this 6-day itinerary includes an essential acclimatization day to give you the best chance of reaching the roof of Africa.

Day 1: Marangu Gate to Mandara Hut

After a morning briefing in Arusha, we drive to the Marangu Gate for registration. Your trek begins through a dense, beautiful rainforest where you can spot blue monkeys and colobus monkeys. We arrive at Mandara Hut (2,700m) in the late afternoon for dinner and your first night in the A-frame cabins.

Day 2: Mandara Hut to Horombo Hut

We leave the rainforest behind and enter the heather and moorland zone. The trail opens up, offering spectacular views of the Kibo and Mawenzi peaks. We trek for about 5 to 6 hours until we reach Horombo Hut (3,720m), which sits in a valley surrounded by giant lobelias.

Day 3: Acclimatization Day at Horombo Hut

This extra day is crucial for your success. We spend the day doing a short acclimatization hike to the fascinating Zebra Rocks (striking volcanic rock formations) before returning to Horombo Hut for a hot lunch and an afternoon of rest.

Day 4: Horombo Hut to Kibo Hut

We trek through the lunar landscape of the "Saddle," a wide alpine desert connecting the Mawenzi and Kibo peaks. We arrive at Kibo Hut (4,700m) at the base of the summit crater wall. We eat an early dinner and try to sleep by 7:00 PM to prepare for the midnight climb.

Day 5: Summit Day! (Kibo Hut to Uhuru Peak to Horombo)

We wake up at midnight, have a hot drink, and begin the steep, grueling climb in the dark. We reach Gilman's Point on the crater rim at dawn, then push along the snow-covered rim to Uhuru Peak (5,895m)—the highest point in Africa! After taking photos, we descend all the way back down to Horombo Hut for a well-deserved sleep.

Day 6: Horombo Hut to Marangu Gate

After a joyful celebration with your porters and guides, we make the long, beautiful descent back through the rainforest to the Marangu Gate. Here, successful climbers receive their summit certificates before we drive you back to Arusha for a hot shower.

Why Choose Marangu?

  • ✓ Hut Accommodations: Sleep in comfortable beds with mattresses and solar lighting (no tents).
  • ✓ Gradual Slope: The path is well-defined and generally less steep than other routes.
  • ✓ Dining Halls: Enjoy your meals sitting comfortably at tables in the communal dining huts.
