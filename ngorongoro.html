Ngorongoro Conservation Area

The Garden of Eden

Overview

The Ngorongoro Crater is a breathtaking natural wonder and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Formed when a massive volcano exploded and collapsed on itself over two million years ago, it is now the largest unbroken caldera in the world. Today, the crater floor is a haven for a dense concentration of wildlife, offering some of the most reliable game viewing in Africa.

The Wildlife Experience

Because the steep crater walls act as a natural enclosure, the majority of the wildlife here does not migrate. You can find almost every species of African plains mammal living in relatively close quarters. It is one of the few places on earth where you have a very high chance of seeing all of the "Big Five" (Lion, Leopard, Elephant, Buffalo, and Rhino) in a single day.

Highlights & Activities

  • ✓ The Endangered Black Rhino: The crater floor is one of the absolute best places in East Africa to spot these rare, magnificent creatures.
  • ✓ Dense Predator Population: The crater boasts an incredibly high density of lions and spotted hyenas, leading to frequent action and sightings.
  • ✓ Maasai Culture: The Ngorongoro Conservation Area is a multiple land-use area where the Maasai people coexist with the wildlife. You can visit traditional Maasai bomas to learn about their way of life.

Best Time to Visit

Year-Round: Wildlife viewing inside the crater is spectacular all year long. However, the dry season (June to October) makes spotting animals easier as the grass is shorter, while the wet season offers stunning green landscapes.
