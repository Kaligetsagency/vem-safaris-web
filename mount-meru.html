Mount Meru

Tanzania's Dramatic Second Summit

Overview

Standing tall at 4,562 meters (14,967 feet), Mount Meru is a dormant stratovolcano located in the heart of Arusha National Park. Often overshadowed by its massive neighbor, Kilimanjaro, Mount Meru is a spectacular trek in its own right. It boasts a dramatic horseshoe-shaped crater, a towering ash cone, and some of the most breathtaking, sheer cliff drops in Africa.

The Trekking Experience

A Mount Meru trek usually takes 3 to 4 days. Because the mountain is located inside a national park teeming with wildlife, the first day of your hike is essentially a walking safari. You will be accompanied by an armed park ranger as you hike past herds of buffalo, giraffes, and warthogs. The upper slopes are steep and challenging, requiring a bit of scrambling along a knife-edge ridge to reach Socialist Peak just in time for sunrise.

Highlights & Activities

  • ✓ The Perfect Acclimatization: Many climbers use Meru as a warm-up for Kilimanjaro to increase their chances of a successful summit.
  • ✓ Wildlife on Foot: Hiking through the lower montane forests allows for close encounters with wildlife and beautiful bird species.
  • ✓ Breathtaking Views: The summit of Mount Meru arguably offers the best possible view of Mount Kilimanjaro rising above the clouds in the distance.

Best Time to Visit

June to October: The dry season is the best time for clear skies and stunning views of Kilimanjaro.
December to February: Another great dry window with slightly warmer temperatures.
