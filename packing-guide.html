The Ultimate Safari Packing List

Travel light, travel smart.

Packing for a Tanzanian safari is all about layers, comfort, and practicality. Internal bush flights have strict luggage weight limits (usually 15kg / 33lbs in soft-sided bags), so packing smart is essential. Here is our expert checklist.

1. Clothing (The Layering System)

Pro Tip: Stick to neutral colors like khaki, olive green, and brown. Avoid dark blue or black, as they can attract tsetse flies, and avoid bright white, which gets dirty quickly.

  • ✓ 3-4 short-sleeve t-shirts or moisture-wicking shirts
  • ✓ 2 long-sleeve shirts (to protect from the sun and evening mosquitoes)
  • ✓ 2 pairs of comfortable, lightweight trousers
  • ✓ 1-2 pairs of shorts
  • ✓ 1 warm fleece jacket or sweater (early morning game drives are chilly!)
  • ✓ 1 lightweight, waterproof windbreaker
  • ✓ Comfortable, closed-toe walking shoes or light hiking boots
  • ✓ A pair of sandals or flip-flops for the lodge/camp
  • ✓ Wide-brimmed safari hat
  • ✓ Swimsuit (many lodges have beautiful pools)

2. Electronics & Gear

  • ✓ Binoculars: Do not share one pair! We recommend 8x42 or 10x42 for the best viewing.
  • ✓ Camera with a good zoom lens (at least 200mm to 300mm) and extra memory cards
  • ✓ Universal travel adapter (Tanzania uses UK-style Type G plugs)
  • ✓ Portable power bank to charge devices in the bush
  • ✓ Headlamp or small flashlight for walking around camp at night

3. Health & Toiletries

  • ✓ High SPF sunscreen and SPF lip balm
  • ✓ Strong insect repellent (containing DEET)
  • ✓ Malaria prophylaxis (consult your doctor before traveling)
  • ✓ Personal first-aid kit (painkillers, anti-diarrhea medication, band-aids, hand sanitizer)
  • ✓ Prescription medications (bring enough for your entire trip in your carry-on)
  • ✓ Wet wipes / baby wipes (great for dusty game drives)

4. Crucial Documents

  • ✓ Passport (valid for at least 6 months after your arrival date)
  • ✓ Printed eVisa (if you applied online)
  • ✓ Yellow Fever Vaccination Certificate (required if traveling from an endemic country)
  • ✓ Comprehensive travel and medical insurance documents
  • ✓ Cash: US Dollars are widely accepted, but bills must be printed after 2013 (older bills are often rejected in East Africa).
